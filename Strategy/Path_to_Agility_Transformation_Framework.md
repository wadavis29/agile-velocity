# Path to Agility Transformation Framework

## Overview: Path to Agility

|       | Align | Learn | Predict | Accelerate | Adapt |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|------------|-------|
| **Org** | Compelling Purpose | Action Enabled | Agile Leadership | Sustaining Improvement | Whole Org Agility |
|       | Rollout Strategy | Team Empowerment | Ability to Focus | Decision Agility | Purpose Driven Leadership |
| **System** | Products Defined | Product Value Management | Ability to Forecast | Faster Time to Value | Market Agility |
|       | Value Alignment | Value Delivery Coordinated | Complexity Reduced | | |
| **Team** | Team Formed | Visibility Created | Predictable Delivery Cadence | Cycle Time Reduced | Product Learning Accelerated |
|       | | Team Ownership | Quality Feedback Loop Shortened | Release Continuously | |

---

## Stages

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Align** | Align the agile initiative with measurable business outcomes and define a clear transformation roadmap. |
| **Learn** | Establish foundational agile practices and a culture of learning by empowering teams to take ownership of their work and process. |
| **Predict** | Maintain a predictable cadence of delivery, enabling organizations to make informed business decisions. |
| **Accelerate** | Optimize the value delivery system and shorten time-to-market. |
| **Adapt** | Embrace organization-wide agility in order to quickly respond to market demands. |

---

## Levels

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Organization** | Leadership develops a modern mindset, increases visibility throughout the organization, and creates alignment around vision, goals, and measurements of success for the organizational change. |
| **System** | Collections of teams are coordinating and collaborating to achieve optimal flow in value delivery. |
| **Team** | Teams successfully take on new roles, establish agile team practices, increase employee engagement, and achieve a sustainable, predictable cadence of delivering value. |

---

## Business Outcomes

| Business Impact | Description | Example Measures |
|-----------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Employee Engagement** | Employees are more satisfied in their work, willing to go the extra mile, passionate about the purpose of their jobs, and committed to the organization. | eNPS, Bad Turnover, Employee Referrals, Offer Acceptance, Feedback Surveys, Team Learning Logs, etc. |
| **Customer Satisfaction** | Customers are satisfied with the experience, benefits, and outcomes when using your product or service. | Number of times a week team members talk to an actual customer, NPS, Retention (DAU/MAU), Referrals, etc. |
| **Quality** | The product or service meets the expectations of the market for usability, reliability, etc. | Defects in production, Change fail percentage, Automated test coverage, Ratio of fixing work vs feature work, Code Coverage |
| **Speed** | The time it takes to deliver an idea into the market. | Cycle time, Lead time, Deployment frequency, Mean time to restore (MTTR), etc. |
| **Predictability** | Teams maintain a predictable cadence of delivery enabling the organization to make informed business decisions. | % Complete of Sprint plan, Velocity variability, Say-Do ratio, % Unplanned work items, etc. |
| **Innovation** | New ideas, creative thoughts, or novel imaginations provide better solutions to meet new requirements, unarticulated needs, or known market needs. | Market Share, Number of validated business-level hypotheses developed and tested, Number of failed experiments, Slack time, etc. |
| **Market Responsiveness** | The ability of the organization to pivot quickly to respond to ever-changing market demands. | AARRR (Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue), HEART (Happiness, engagement, adoption, retention, task success), etc. |
| **Productivity** | Increase the business value realized while maintaining or reducing costs. | Value delivered, ROI, KPIs, Focus and flow - "Thieves of time," etc. |
| **Continuous Improvement** | The ability of the organization to relentlessly pursue optimizations in all aspects of business functions. | Value Delivery System flow efficiency increases, Reduction in recurring impediments, Cumulative Flow, etc. |

## Agile Outcomes

### AO-001: Compelling Purpose (1-Align, Org)
**Description:** The organization is aligned and motivated by a clear organizational purpose, vision, set of objectives, and a compelling purpose for change and improvement.

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Continuous Improvement

---

### AO-002: Rollout Strategy (1-Align, Org)
**Description:** An Agile Leadership Team has been established and a transformation roadmap defined, taking into account new organization structure demands, top risks, and incremental rollout.

**Predecessor:** Compelling Purpose

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Continuous Improvement

---

### AO-003: Products Defined (1-Align, System)
**Description:** Products are clearly defined, understood, and are serving as a driver for organizing the system and teams.

**Predecessors:** Rollout Strategy, Compelling Purpose

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Employee Engagement, Quality, Market Responsiveness, Customer Satisfaction

---

### AO-004: Value Alignment (1-Align, System)
**Description:** System of teams is organized around value to deliver work with minimal outside dependencies, tightly aligning the relationship between delivery and the customer/stakeholders.

**Predecessor:** Products Defined

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Employee Engagement, Quality, Productivity, Market Responsiveness, Innovation, Customer Satisfaction

---

### AO-005: Team Formed (1-Align, Team)
**Description:** Teams know their stakeholders and how their work contributes to the larger goals (e.g. System and Organization), they have agreed on how they work together, and team roles are understood and filled.

**Predecessor:** Value Alignment

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Employee Engagement, Quality

---

### AO-006: Action Enabled (2-Learn, Org)
**Description:** The Agile Leadership Team is facilitating the overall transformation, resolving organizational impediments with urgency, and continuing to communicate the change vision, wins, and learnings. Measurable progress is being communicated and people are taking action.

**Predecessor:** Rollout Strategy

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Continuous Improvement

---

### AO-007: Team Empowerment (2-Learn, Org)
**Description:** Leaders are shifting to team-based responsibility and performance over individual results by supporting team autonomy, building a continual learning and improvement culture, and demonstrating their own agile leadership in the ways they lead (communicate, behave, and think).

**Predecessors:** Value Alignment, Compelling Purpose

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Employee Engagement, Continuous Improvement, Speed, Innovation

---

### AO-008: Product Value Management (2-Learn, System)
**Description:** There is clear product management responsibility established and customers/stakeholders are engaged partners in sequencing work along with ongoing evaluation of product success.

**Predecessor:** Products Defined

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Predictability, Productivity, Market Responsiveness, Customer Satisfaction

---

### AO-009: Value Delivery Coordinated (2-Learn, System)
**Description:** Teams are working together to deliver value and manage handoffs/dependencies across the system.

**Predecessors:** Value Alignment, Visibility Created

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Continuous Improvement, Predictability, Speed, Productivity

---

### AO-010: Visibility Created (2-Learn, Team)
**Description:** All the work a team has planned, in progress, and done is visible and reviewed regularly with engaged stakeholders.

**Predecessor:** Team Formed

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Predictability

---

### AO-011: Team Ownership (2-Learn, Team)
**Description:** The team is self-organizing to collaboratively take ownership of their work and improving continuously to deliver value more effectively.

**Predecessors:** Team Formed, Visibility Created, Team Empowerment

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Employee Engagement, Continuous Improvement, Quality, Predictability, Innovation

---

### AO-012: Agile Leadership (3-Predict, Org)
**Description:** Leaders are creating the necessary environment for success by offering clear vision and goals, guiding and developing their people, removing team obstacles, moving decision-making to those closest to the work, optimizing the end-to-end system, and promoting craftsmanship and high quality among their teams.

**Predecessor:** Team Empowerment

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Employee Engagement, Quality, Innovation

---

### AO-013: Ability to Focus (3-Predict, Org)
**Description:** Leaders are articulating value, ensuring strategic alignment, sequencing the work at the appropriate level, and then allowing the next layer to pull and decompose, so teams can focus on delivering the highest value work.

**Predecessors:** Agile Leadership, Ability to Forecast, Product Value Management

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Employee Engagement, Quality, Predictability, Speed, Productivity, Customer Satisfaction

---

### AO-014: Ability to Forecast (3-Predict, System)
**Description:** System and product planning is continually forecasted based on available and relevant data in partnership with stakeholders to deliver the highest incremental value with clear visibility to enable informed trade-off decision making.

**Predecessors:** Product Value Management, Complexity Reduced, Predictable Delivery Cadence, Value Delivery Coordinated

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Predictability

---

### AO-015: Complexity Reduced (3-Predict, System)

**Predecessor:** Agile Leadership

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Quality, Predictability, Productivity, Innovation

### AO-016: Predictable Delivery Cadence (3-Predict, Team)

### AO-017: Quality Feedback Loop Shortened (3-Predict, Team)

**Predecessor:** Team Ownership

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Quality, Predictability, Speed, Productivity, Customer Satisfaction

---

**Predecessor:** Action Enabled

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Continuous Improvement

### AO-019: Decision Agility (4-Accelerate, Org)

### AO-020: Faster Time to Value (4-Accelerate, System)

**Predecessors:** Cycle Time Reduced, Complexity Reduced, Decision Agility

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Speed, Productivity, Market Responsiveness, Innovation, Customer Satisfaction

---

### AO-021: Cycle Time Reduced (4-Accelerate, Team)

**Predecessors:** Predictable Delivery Cadence, Release Continuously

**Predecessors:** Decision Agility, Sustaining Improvement

**Business Outcomes Impacted:** Innovation

---

**Description:** Leaders ensure a clear purpose and foster an environment, inspiring, aligning, and empowering teams to be innovative and adaptive.

**Predecessors:** Decision Agility, Whole Org Agility

---

**Description:** Anticipate, sense, and respond to changing market needs with short market validation cycles.

**Predecessors:** Cycle Time Reduced, Release Continuously

## Capabilities

### Align Stage Capabilities

#### C-001: Org Vision (Compelling Purpose)
**Description:** The vision describes the organization's purpose and what it aspires to be.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- The vision inspires and motivates the organization
- The vision aligns the organization to a common goal
- The vision is clear and everyone in the organization can speak to it
- The vision informs the strategic themes of the organization

---

#### C-002: Org Objectives (Compelling Purpose)
**Description:** Clear organizational objectives have been defined with measurable success criteria.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Organizational objectives are defined in alignment with the vision and strategy
- Clear business objectives have been articulated and agreed upon by organization leadership
- The business objectives and measures have been broadly shared with the organization
- Alignment amongst the whole organization and stakeholders exists
- Measurable success criteria for the objectives is defined (e.g., specific, quantifiable, measurable, compelling)
- The organization's members understand how they contribute to the organizational objectives

---

#### C-003: Urgency to Change (Compelling Purpose)
**Description:** A compelling reason why the organization should change has been communicated across the organization, clear measures of success have been defined, and everyone is compelled into action.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- It is clear to all why it is imperative that the organization must change
- A guiding vision that will focus and provide clarity of mission for the transformation approach has been created
- Clear business objectives have been defined with measurable success criteria
- The change vision and objectives have been broadly communicated across the organization and alignment achieved
- Everyone is encouraged to share when progress has been made towards achieving the vision
- Organization is motivated to work on the change

---

#### C-004: Agile Leadership Team (ALT) Formed (Rollout Strategy)
**Description:** A powerful Guiding Coalition is formed with the ability to drive change throughout the organization as needed.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- The Agile Leadership Team has been identified
- The Agile Leadership Team is a real, powerful coalition with decision making powers
- All Agile Leadership Team roles have been filled (PO, SM, Coach, Stakeholders, Team Members)
- Working agreements are defined (cadence, framework, backlog, etc.)
- The Agile Leadership Team has communicated to everyone involved in the transformation
- Ownership of the transformation is shared with active representation and support across the organization (i.e. Business & IT)

---

#### C-005: Agile Organizational Structure (Rollout Strategy)
**Description:** The impact to organizational structure (new roles, team design, management structure, etc.) is understood and an initial plan is in place to implement.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- The rationale and potential structure of an agile organization is well understood within Agile Leadership Team
- Delta between current organization structure and an agile structure is understood
- Initial plan in place to move from current structure to agile structure
- Initial plan includes obtaining corporate-level (HR, finance, etc.) support
- Commitment made to move to new agile structure
- People have been assigned to new roles for initial rollout scope

---

#### C-006: Communication Plan (Rollout Strategy)
**Description:** A communication plan is created to communicate the change vision, objectives, and rollout strategy including ongoing communication channels.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- How - Leaders have examples of effective communications (eg. FAQ, scripts, etc.)
- Where - Communication channels are defined (eg. town halls, slack)
- Who - Senders and audiences are defined (eg. Stakeholders, people impacted)
- When - Cadence of communication is defined (e.g consistency, to reinforce quick wins)
- What - Initial content is prepared including standard presentation formats
- An ongoing communication plan is defined

---

#### C-007: Mechanism to Measure Progress (Rollout Strategy)
**Description:** There is a plan in place to measure and communicate progress towards the transformation goals.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Gaps have been identified from current data collection mechanisms for capturing data associated with the transformation progress
- These gaps are filled with new mechanisms to collect data associated with the transformation progress
- Progress reporting cadence and format has been defined

---

#### C-008: Transformation Backlog (Rollout Strategy)
**Description:** A prioritized list of the items (impediments, changes, decisions, etc.) identified for the Agile Leadership Team to work from is visible.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Initial transformation backlog created based on what the organization needs to accomplish the transformation vision
- Backlog items are well formed with clear Acceptance Criteria
- Backlog is prioritized by Agile Leadership Team Product Owner with coaching guidance
- Prioritized backlog is publicly displayed and broadly communicated to the entire organization

---

#### C-009: Transformation Roadmap (Rollout Strategy)
**Description:** A high-level roadmap is in place outlining initial teams and how the Agile change will spread throughout the organization.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Rollout plan created, showing a high-level view of the big steps involved in the transformation and the approximate time frames
- Plan is shared with everyone involved in the transformation
- Training/Education plan created (i.e. who, when, what)

---

#### C-010: Product Vision and Scope (Products Defined)
**Description:** Product(s) scope, definition, and business strategy alignment are clear to stakeholders and teams. The Product Vision defines the potential external market opportunity or internal landscape and how the product will serve those needs.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Product(s) scope, definition, boundaries, and business strategy alignment is clear to stakeholders and teams
- Key indicators of success are identified, agreed upon, and measurable
- The product vision defines the potential external market opportunity or internal landscape and how the product will serve those needs
- Product scope boundaries are clear
- The measures of how the product supports business strategy are defined
- Teams understand value and can trace their work to product success
- Components of the product have been identified
- Product strategy is aligned to business strategy
- Competitive landscape is understood
- Market positioning is defined

---

#### C-011: Customer and Market Clarity (Products Defined)
**Description:** The customer(s) are defined in order to enable consistent alignment and value delivery. There is clarity on the challenges faced by customers.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- The customers are known and understood
- The stakeholders are known and understood
- Customer segments and characteristics are understood
- Customer needs and challenges are explicitly documented
- Explicit value drivers are defined and used in value attribution of product opportunities
- There is clarity on the challenges faced by customers
- Customer usage scenarios are explicitly documented and considered in defining product capabilities

---

#### C-012: Ownership of the Product Defined (Products Defined)
**Description:** The organization has clarified and fulfilled the Product Management roles.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- The roles that support the product have been defined (e.g. Product Management/Product Owner)
- The organization has a plan to fill the product roles (e.g. Product Management/Product Owner)
- There is a plan to address known gaps between role expectations and current role capabilities

---

#### C-013: Clear Value Definition (Value Alignment)
**Description:** The needs of the market and/or stakeholders define the team's purpose and guide value delivery.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Key stakeholder or market success drivers are known
- Team members can articulate their team's value proposition
- Measures of success are defined

---

#### C-014: Value Delivery Systems Identified (Value Alignment)
**Description:** The ways in which value is delivered and how it flows are understood.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- All places where work originates have been identified
- Incoming work is characterized to identify major types, groupings, percentages, etc.
- All steps to complete that work have been identified
- All places where work is delivered have been identified

---

#### C-015: Teams Aligned to Value (Value Alignment)
**Description:** Cross-functional teams have been defined for the value delivery systems with minimal dependencies and handoffs.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Clear roles and responsibilities have been defined to manage the value delivery system
- Skills and people needed to complete different types of work have been determined
- Cross-functional teams have been defined
- Teams have been defined appropriately to deliver value in targeted value delivery system
- Team grouping minimizes dependencies and handoffs outside of the value delivery system
- Consensus is achieved on the new org model aligned around value

---

#### C-016: Clear Team Purpose (Team Formed)
**Description:** Team has clearly defined goals with an aligned set of expectations enabling autonomy. They understand how their work ties into the larger whole.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Team members are able to articulate the value their team is chartered to provide
- Team members understand the customer-focused vision their work helps the larger organization realize
- Team members understand, can articulate, and played a part in the creation of their Team's mission to help realize the larger vision
- The Team understands the factors that make up their success

---

#### C-017: Stakeholders Identified (Team Formed)
**Description:** The team should know who has a stake in the outcome of the products they are building and who sets the direction.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- The team understands who its stakeholders are
- The team understands the difference between those who are "Interested" and those who are "Invested"
- Team members understand and are empowered to redirect stakeholders to the appropriate roles, e.g., Product Owner or Scrum Master

---

#### C-018: Roles Fulfilled (Team Formed)
**Description:** Team roles and responsibilities are clearly defined, aligned, and understood by all.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Team members understand the roles
- Team members understand the responsibilities of each role
- Team members understand their new role, or how their existing role has changed
- Team members understand the roles other members of their team have in their new way of working
- Each role is fulfilled

---

#### C-019: Working Agreements (Team Formed)
**Description:** Setting expectations around the way members of a team prefer to work together creates a foundation of openness and accountability from the start. These should be regularly revisited.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- The Team has working agreements published for how they will work and collaborate
- The Teams' values are defined and agreed upon (courage, listening, edification, etc.)
- The Team agrees on the process and events it will follow
- The Team agrees on the criteria for a backlog item to be considered Done
- The Team has a clear definition for what criteria must be met to be ready to start work on a backlog item

---

### Learn Stage Capabilities

#### C-020: Communicate Wins (Action Enabled)
**Description:** Momentum and excitement for the transformation is sustained through constant communication of positive progress towards achieving the transformation goals.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Transformation progress is communicated regularly to the entire organization
- Wins are publicized (visible, unambiguous, clearly related to the change initiative)
- Credibility and momentum in the agile transformation is established

---

#### C-021: Urgent Organizational Impediment Removal (Action Enabled)
**Description:** Organizational impediments are being surfaced, made visible, prioritized, and resolved quickly.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- There is a clear process for teams to surface organizational impediments
- Organizational impediments are made visible as reported to the entire organization
- Organizational impediments are clearly prioritized
- Leadership resolves impediments with urgency based on priority

---

#### C-022: Measure Agile Transformation Progress (Action Enabled)
**Description:** Progress towards achieving the transformation goals and organizational agility are being measured and reported.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Data is being collected regarding the transformation measures of success
- Ability to assess progress of team level agile adoption established
- Ability to assess overall progress towards organizational agility established

---

#### C-023: Effective Agile Leadership Team (ALT) (Action Enabled)
**Description:** The Agile Leadership Team is walking the talk by running the transformation with agile resulting in quick wins and building momentum.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- The Agile Leadership Team has a clear prioritized backlog
- The Agile Leadership Team's work progress is made visible to the entire organization
- The Agile Leadership Team is meeting on a regular cadence with active participation from team members
- The Agile Leadership Team has a cadence of planning (~2wks)
- The Agile Leadership Team has a cadence of retrospecting (~2wks)
- The Agile Leadership Team has established momentum in completing items from their backlog

---

#### C-024: Team Responsibility (Team Empowerment)
**Description:** Leaders and managers shift expectations from individual performance and outcomes to supporting and encouraging whole-team performance measures and outcomes.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Leadership understands the scope of each team's responsibility and how impediments should be escalated when they fall outside a team's scope of responsibility
- Leaders hold teams responsible for maximizing the business value they deliver, and their ability to deliver incrementally in the form of potentially shippable product increments
- Teams hold the larger organization responsible for creating the environment for their success
- Team members are evaluated not only by their mastery of their domains of expertise, but also by their ability to work as part of a high-functioning team

---

#### C-025: Team Autonomy (Team Empowerment)
**Description:** If teams are to be held accountable for hitting their goals, they need to have everything to accomplish those goals and a clear way to advocate for as much autonomy as possible.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Teams have all environments, access, permissions, space, and similar needed to deliver business value on a consistent basis. When this isn't the case, there is clear definition of how to escalate their needs to the appropriate people with the authority and availability to take action
- Team autonomy is clearly defined, and both teams and leaders understand the scope of a team's autonomy
- Team autonomy is regularly reviewed and revised, with a preference toward increasing team autonomy whenever possible

---

#### C-026: Culture of Learning (Team Empowerment)
**Description:** Creating a solid learning loop of inspecting and adapting is the secret sauce to continuous improvement. If a team doesn't have the safety to try something new for fear of slowing down, breaking something, or other repercussions, they aren't going to excel. Teams own the evaluation and mitigation of risk and balance that risk with experiments to validate hypotheses.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Teams have slack (available time) to learn
- Teams are encouraged to take risks in order to learn and report on the progress of their learnings
- Team success is measured both in terms of value delivered and their improvement based on learnings
- Leadership emphasizes learning in visible tangible ways

---

#### C-027: Awareness of Leadership Shift (Team Empowerment)
**Description:** Leaders are aware and educated on their needed leadership shift and journey to leadership agility.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Leaders are trained/educated on the new ways of thinking and working
- Leaders understand the role of an agile leader (at any level)
- Leaders understand why team empowerment matters and how they can influence it
- Leaders understand the gaps between agile leadership expectations and current capabilities

---

#### C-028: Management of Product Success (Product Value Management)
**Description:** Key indicators of success are identified, agreed upon, and measurable.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Measurement is being made on product success metrics and is used to inform action
- Economic outcomes are being monitored
- Product Health is being measured (i.e. satisfaction, flow, etc.)
- Clear objectives are maintained and visible
- Leading and lagging indicators of product effectiveness/adoption are explicitly defined
- A customer feedback loop has been established, and we are listening to what customers are saying
- Measures of successful value creation are clear and have been communicated, and the metrics are available
- The way the product generates a return for the company (Financial Model) is clear

---

#### C-029: Product Prioritization (Product Value Management)
**Description:** The organization has communicated clear priorities that align with product vision and success factors.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- The Backlog of the product is visible and sequenced across the system of teams
- A product roadmap is developed and maintained
- Prioritization is influenced by various sources (i.e. stakeholders, customers, teams, etc.)
- Backlog prioritization considers short term vs long term trade offs
- A mechanism for managing and prioritizing the reduction of technical debt exists

---

#### C-030: Customer/Stakeholder Engagement (Product Value Management)
**Description:** Customer and stakeholder wants and needs are understood at a level of detail that enables effective trade-off decisions.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- A clear intake process is defined for new requests
- Bi-directional communication is established
- Stakeholders and internal customers are partners with the delivery teams and engaged in system demos
- System level demos are conducted on a standard cadence at least monthly

---

#### C-031: Product Function Established (Product Value Management)
**Description:** Product Managers demonstrate their empowerment to drive product success.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Roles are defined and fulfilled
- Product Managers/Owners have authority
- Product Managers/Owners have time
- Product Managers/Owners have domain knowledge
- Product Managers/Owners have skills
- Product Managers/Owners trained/educated on their new role

---

#### C-032: System Ownership (Value Delivery Coordinated)
**Description:** Establish a mechanism for shared ownership across teams to enable the flow of value and continuous improvement on a regular cadence.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- System roles and responsibilities are fulfilled and known
- Teams and System Roles are educated on scaling techniques
- An activity for cross-team lessons learned is facilitated on a regular cadence
- Actions are owned and a strategy for adoption is identified
- Mechanisms for cross-team collaboration & problem-solving are activated, resulting in system level improvements

---

#### C-033: Cross-Team Coordination (Value Delivery Coordinated)
**Description:** Create awareness of the big picture and ability for all contributors and stakeholders to see their role in coordinated value delivery, allowing for improved dependency management and decision-making for the benefit of the whole system.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Teams are clear on how their priorities and work relate to the whole system
- Teams understand their dependencies and impacts on other teams
- Team level decisions take into account their impact on the overall Team of Teams' efforts
- Teams come together at a regular cadence to plan and coordinate upcoming work
- The plan is visible to all teams and stakeholders
- Various events are scheduled to ensure ongoing coordination of the cross-team initiatives (e.g. Big Room Planning, Product Owner Syncs, Scrum of Scrums, System Demos, etc.)

---

#### C-034: Ability to Measure (Value Delivery Coordinated)
**Description:** The pace and progress of value delivery is easily measured at team, system, and portfolio levels.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Team level work is traceable to system level objectives
- Data is collected on system-level aspects such as average lead times, flow, and accumulated delivery of value
- Metrics associated with collected data are defined and are used to inform action

---

#### C-035: Make Work Visible (Visibility Created)
**Description:** The best decisions are made when the most information is held by the most people. Making work visible helps all necessary parties receive the necessary data.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- All work is captured in the backlog
- All of the team's work is accounted for on a visibility board (physical or electronic)

---

#### C-036: Product Backlog (Visibility Created)
**Description:** A list of prioritized and well defined deliverables provides a team with the focus to be the most effective.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Everything in the upcoming planning increment is sized
- Items are ordered by current priority
- Items are vertically sliced to deliver business value
- Backlog is decomposed appropriately (smaller/more detailed towards the top, larger/vaguer towards the bottom)
- Refinement is consistently happening to keep the backlog fresh and updated

---

#### C-037: Stakeholder Engagement (Visibility Created)
**Description:** Stakeholder alignment reduces waste and increases delivered value. Get stakeholder input early and often to reduce confusion and potential rework.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- The Product Owner communicates regularly with each stakeholder and seeks out their advice for Product Backlog content and priority based on vision and success criteria of the team/product
- Team demonstrates progress and gets feedback from stakeholders every iteration
- Stakeholders have visibility into how the team is tracking towards planning increment/release

#### C-098: Outcome Driven Experimentation (Product Learning Accelerated)
**Description:** Teams embrace uncertainty, creating hypotheses and executing experiments to (in)validate assumptions quickly.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- Teams can pivot and respond to change, without excessive ramp down/ramp up effort

- Implementation is defined as experiments with clear learning parameters

#### Predictable Delivery Cadence

**Potentially Shippable Product Increment:**
- Define the definition of done - Clearly define what constitutes a potentially shippable product increment.
- Schedule regular increment reviews - Plan and schedule regular reviews to demonstrate potentially shippable increments.
- Ensure business value alignment - Confirm that each increment provides tangible business value to the customer.

**Forecasting:**
- Collect historical data - Gather and analyze past data on team performance, such as velocity and throughput.
- Create forecasting models - Use historical data to forecast future delivery.
- Provide forecasting coaching or training - Offer coaching or training on how to create forecasts based on roadmaps, backlog items, estimates and empirical data.
- Share forecasting results - Communicate the forecast and supporting data/rationale to stakeholders to set realistic expectations.
- Refine forecasting techniques - Regularly review and improve forecasting techniques based on accuracy and feedback.

**Limit WIP:**
- Set WIP limits - Collaboratively agree on work-in-progress (WIP) limits that are supported by data and make them visible.
- Track and review WIP - Establish a process for regularly tracking and reviewing actual WIP against agreed limits.
- Pull work, don't push it - Pull new work when downstream capacity becomes available rather than pushing work into a queue or overburdening downstream work.
- Offer coaching support on developing and utilizing WIP limits - Setting WIP limits and using them to effectively deliver faster and with less variability is often counterintuitive and very challenging for teams and management.
- Implement single-tasking strategies - Introduce strategies to promote single-tasking and reduce context switching.

**Predictable Throughput:**
- Measure say-do ratio - Implement a process for measuring actual delivery, relative to what was planned.
- Analyze historical data - Use historical data, rather than wishful thinking, to inform planning and improve predictability.
- Make planning a core discipline - Incorporate historical data, emergent work, market forces, dependencies and practical considerations to make planning a continuous activity and skill.
- Introduce sizing techniques - Train the team on sizing techniques to create a common currency for sizing and help the team learn to ask clarifying questions and create a shared understanding of the work.

- Develop cross-training plans - Create plans for cross-training team members to expand their expertise and provide the team with more flexibility.

**Cross-Functional Team:**

- Develop shared specialist strategies - Create strategies for working with shared specialists across teams, work this into mid-range planning and communicate trade-off decisions.
- Promote E-shaped skills - Encourage team members to develop E-shaped skills, giving them deep expertise in multiple areas and also interdisciplinary skills allowing them to collaborate effectively with others.

**Continuous Integration:**
- Automate builds and tests - Set up automated build and testing processes to provide quick feedback.
- Monitor CI health - Regularly monitor and address issues in the CI process looking for delays and integration challenges.
- Train the team on CI practices - Provide training on effective continuous integration practices.

**Test Automation:**

- Regularly maintain the test suite - Schedule regular reviews to validate, prune, and maintain the automated test suite.

### Accelerate Stage Recommendations

#### Sustaining Improvement

- Define the coaching engagement model - Develop and document how the organization can identify needs and engage the coaching team, setting expectations about how and when coaches will interact with teams, departments and leaders.

- Develop Agile fundamentals - Agree upon and document the Agile practices that require consistency across the organization for effective scaling.

**Continuing Education:**

- Develop an education support plan - Create a plan that offers educational support tailored to different roles, teams, and leadership levels.
- Create an evolving education catalog - Establish a process for regularly updating the education catalog to address emerging knowledge gaps and new developments in the industry.
- Provide education opportunities that not only support cross-training but also support role-specific career path development - Plan and conduct ongoing education sessions to support continuous learning across the organization.

**Ongoing Knowledge Exchange:**

**Leverage Outcome-Based Metrics:**
- Define outcome-based metrics - Based on specific business or technical goals, define the metrics that will inform decision makers on progress toward the goals and inform trade-offs.
- Establish baselines - If you want to measure the result of meeting goals, you have to first be able to measure where you're at today.
- Implement mechanisms for capturing data - Set up tools and/or processes to capture relevant data related to the defined goals.
- Create transparency around progress - Develop a system to make progress on these metrics visible to all stakeholders.
- Bring data into the decision-making process - Ensure data is understood and relevant, then make that data part of the decision-making process.
- Evaluate and iterate on metrics - Schedule regular reviews to evaluate the metrics against baselines and goals, making adjustments as necessary.

**Create Alignment with Clear Objectives:**
- Define organizational objectives - Define organizational objectives in clear, unambiguous terms that have measurable results.
- Create team and/or individual goals - Employees should be able to set their own team or personal goals that align with the higher level organizational objectives.
- Communicate organizational objectives - Ensure that organizational objectives are shared broadly and repeatedly.

**Decentralize Decision Making:**
- Define what types of decisions will be centralized vs. decentralized - Define what types of decisions will be centralized and which will be decentralized.
- Communicate decision authority - Ensure that decision-making authority (and the framework for it) is well communicated and understood across the organization.
- Practice decision transparency - Make decisions transparent by explaining the rationale and the intent.
- Monitor progress - Get feedback periodically and pay close attention to whether or not management is overriding or preventing decision-making at lower levels.

#### Faster Time to Value

**Optimizing the Whole:**
- "Value" is defined - Consider what your end customer would consider as the value being delivered.
- Define the value delivery system - Map out the entire end-to-end value delivery system, identifying all major components, steps, supporting applications and stakeholders.
- Align teams to value - "Bring the work to the team" is a core concept.
- Value delivery measures are in place - Set up metrics to measure the effectiveness of the entire value delivery system, making them visible to all stakeholders.
- Optimization opportunities reviewed regularly - Create a regular cadence for reviewing the value delivery system, focusing on identifying areas for improvement.

**Minimum Viable Delivery:**

- Engage customers for validation - Set up processes for early and ongoing customer validation of each product increment.
- Align team around minimum delivery - Organize a team workshop to build consensus on the importance of focusing on the minimum viable delivery.
- Create a repeatable feedback loop - Implement a feedback loop where customer insights from early iterations inform future increments.

#### Cycle Time Reduced

**Focus to Finish:**
- Set and enforce WIP limits - Define and agree upon clear work-in-progress (WIP) limits for the team to focus on finishing tasks before starting new ones.
- Implement a swarming strategy - Develop and practice a swarming strategy where the entire team works together to complete items in progress.
- Focus on finishing - Shift focus of daily huddle/stand-up on what it will take to complete each work item.
- Track and celebrate small wins - Monitor the completion of shippable increments and celebrate these achievements to reinforce the focus on finishing.

**Deliver Small Increments of Value:**
- Break down work into smaller functional slices - Identify and decompose work into smaller, manageable slices that deliver incremental business value.
- Minimize context switching - Implement guidelines to reduce context switching and maintain focus on the current task.
- Prioritize minimally viable deliverables - Ensure the team focuses on delivering the smallest viable solution that provides value to the business.
- Conduct a goal alignment workshop - Hold a workshop to ensure all team members understand and are committed to the common goal.

**Skill Flexibility:**

- Create cross-functional task assignments - Assign tasks in a way that allows team members to apply multiple skills and reduce reliance on single points of failure.
- Identify and address skill gaps - Conduct a skills assessment to identify gaps and create a plan to address them through training or mentoring.
- Facilitate knowledge-sharing sessions - Organize regular sessions where team members share their skills and knowledge with others.
- Establish a skill diversification plan - Develop a plan for each team member to acquire new skills that align with team priorities.

**Emergent Design:**

- Establish continuous refactoring practices - Implement practices that encourage developers to refactor and improve code continuously.
- Create a tech debt reduction plan - Develop a plan that outlines how the team will systematically reduce technical debt over time.

- Establish deployment standards - Define and document the standards for production-quality deployments, ensuring that all deployed code meets these criteria.

- Develop remediation strategies - Create and document remediation strategies for potential production impacts.

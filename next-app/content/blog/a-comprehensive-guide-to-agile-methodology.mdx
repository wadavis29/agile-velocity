## What is Agile Methodology? Definition and Business Benefits

The Agile Methodology was originally developed for the software industry to manage complex projects more effectively. It's a way to manage projects where requirements and solutions often evolve along with the project's life cycle.

Unlike traditional project management methodologies -[such as Waterfall](https://www.withintent.com/blog/agile-vs-waterfall/?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=19749382271&utm_content=145187142134&utm_term=&utm_term=&utm_campaign=Dynamic+Search+Ads+-+USA+%7C+Website+traffic+%7C+1.03.2023&utm_source=adwords&utm_medium=ppc&hsa_acc=4531781191&hsa_cam=19749382271&hsa_grp=145187142134&hsa_ad=649670678106&hsa_src=g&hsa_tgt=dsa-19959388920&hsa_kw=&hsa_mt=&hsa_net=adwords&hsa_ver=3&gad_source=1&gclid=CjwKCAjww_iwBhApEiwAuG6ccMMgeYcN1mM19JxRuru7W6gg404DEOaAZ0WBbb6KOQ9TeWG4sS1rtxoCKBAQAvD_BwE) - where every stage of the project is planned ahead and executed in a linear fashion, Agile embraces change and allows more flexibility with circular feedback loops.

In Agile, projects are broken down into small, manageable chunks known as iterations or sprints. Each sprint delivers a usable part of the final product and is typically completed in a fixed, short time-frame. This incremental and iterative approach ensures [continuous improvement](insights/blog/mastering-continuous-improvement-in-agile), allowing the project team to respond to changes quickly and effectively, even late in the development process.

In the following sections we'll take a deeper dive into the Agile approach to give you a robust foundation of the principles, frameworks, roles and processes, as well as, tools and techniques for execution.

### Core Agile Methodology Principles That Drive Transformation

![Agile principles illustrated with data visualization and team collaboration concepts.](/images/blog/Agile-Principles-Illustration.jpg)

[The Agile Manifesto](https://www.techtarget.com/searchcio/definition/Agile-Manifesto#:~:text=The%20Agile%20Manifesto%20is%20a,during%20an%20outing%20on%20Feb.), penned in 2001 by a group of software developers, has significantly influenced modern project management practices. This seminal document outlines four key values and twelve principles that underpin the Agile approach.

#### The Four Key Values of the Agile Manifesto--Emphasize:
- Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
- Working software over comprehensive documentation
- Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
- Responding to change over following a plan

These tenets highlight a cultural shift from the rigidity of traditional methodologies to a more dynamic and responsive project management style.

Twelve Core Agile Manifesto Principles:

- Customer satisfaction through early and continuous delivery of valuable work.
- Welcoming changing requirements, even later in the project.
- Frequent delivery of workable software.
- Collaboration with customers throughout the project.
- Motivated teams are given the support they need and are trusted to get the job done.
- Face-to-face conversation is the best way to communicate.
- Delivering working software is the primary measure of progress.
- Agile processes promote sustainable work.
- Attention to excellence and good design.
- Simplicity is key - the art of maximizing the amount of work not done is essential.
- The best architectures, requirements, and designs emerge from self-organizing teams.
- Regular reflection on how to become more effective and adjust behaviors accordingly.

### Leading Agile Frameworks for Scalable Business Impact

![Agile framework illustration depicting team collaboration with charts and boards.](/images/blog/Agile-Frameworks-Illustration.jpg)

#### Agile Team Structure

The Agile team is structured to foster a collaborative environment where [cross-functional team members](/blog/improve-cross-functional-collaboration) work together towards a common goal. The composition of an Agile team often includes a mix of roles, depending on the type of businesses or organization.

Real-World Example--A Diverse Software Development Team

For example, a software development team might include a mix of developers, testers, designers, and a product owner who collectively possess the skills necessary to complete a project from start to finish. This cross-functional approach enables the team to respond swiftly to changes and deliver value more efficiently.

### Agile Events

Agile events like daily stand-ups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives, play a critical role in maintaining the rhythm and momentum of the team. These structured interactions ensure continuous communication, enable the team to reflect on their processes, and provide opportunities for regular progress assessments.

Emphasizing continuous improvement, Agile teams [strive to enhance their workflows and practices](insights/blog/measuring-team-health-and-morale-a-guide-to-improving-your-workplace) to achieve higher performance and deliver quality results consistently.

### Agile Frameworks

While Agile is a philosophy, there are [numerous frameworks](/blog/choosing-the-right-agile-framework) within Agile Methodology that provide a set of practices and tools to implement this philosophy more effectively. Some popular ones include:

- Scrum: This popular framework is commonly used to manage complex projects. It uses short iterations called sprints for development, typically lasting two weeks.
- Extreme Programming (XP): XP is best suited for software development with rapidly changing requirements. It focuses on high customer involvement, continuous feedback, and process improvement.
- Kanban: Originally derived from lean manufacturing, Kanban is used to improve efficiency. It uses boards and cards to visualize the workflow, limit work in progress, and continuously improve processes.
- Lean: Lean methodology emphasizes maximizing customer value and minimizing waste.
- SAFe速: This is a scaled Agile framework used for scaling Agile processes, which many teams struggle to do.
- LeSS: With this framework for a scaling scrum to multiple teams, all using one Sprint to deliver on a single product.

### Key Agile Roles for Successful Transformation Initiatives

![Agile team roles and responsibilities illustrated through collaboration and communication.](/images/blog/Agile-Roles-and-Responsibilities-Illustration.jpg)

Teams who adopt Agile methodologies can experience [a significant boost in efficiency](insights/blog/enabling-agile-team-performance-with-path-to-agility) due to the emphasis on iterative development and continuous feedback.

Agile practices foster a [culture of collaboration](/blog/breaking-down-silos-foster-collaboration), allowing teams to work more cohesively and respond quickly to changes. This flexibility often leads to an enhanced final product that more closely aligns with customer needs.

### Key Agile Roles

In an Agile environment, [project management roles](/blog/agile-roles-your-business-must-hire) evolve from traditional command-and-control leaders to facilitators who work together to empower the team.

They must guide the team through Agile processes, ensuring clear communication and a shared understanding of goals. They also help the team maintain a focus on delivering value during each iteration, balancing scope, and prioritization with stakeholder expectations.

In an Agile project, there are typically three key roles that are fundamental to the successful implementation of Agile Methodology:

#### Product Owner:
The [Product Owner](/blog/technical-skills-product-owner) is essentially the voice of the customer or stakeholder within the project. They have a clear understanding of the product and the customer's requirements and needs. Their main responsibility is to [communicate the vision](/blog/invest-dedicated-product-owner) of the product to the team. They prioritize work based on business value and are accountable for ensuring the team delivers value to the business. Their tasks involve managing and prioritizing the product backlog, deciding the release date and content, accepting or rejecting work results.

#### Scrum Master:
The [Scrum Master](/blog/what-does-a-scrummaster-do-anyway) acts as a facilitator and coach for the Agile team, rather than a traditional project manager. They do not manage the team but help remove any obstacles or blockers that are holding the team back and ensuring the team adheres to the Agile principles and values. They facilitate meetings, deal with challenges, and help the team maintain their work progress report. The Scrum Master works closely with the Product Owner to make sure the product backlog is ready for the next sprint.

#### Development Team:
The Development Team is composed of [professionals who carry out the work](/blog/agile-team-structure) of delivering the product increments. These are cross-functional members who design, analyze, test, and develop the project. The team is self-organizing, meaning they decide on how to complete the work in a sprint.

In [larger organizations](/blog/scaling-agile-across-multiple-teams), there might be additional roles such as [Agile Coach](/blog/8-roles-agile-coaches-play) (who mentors and coaches the organization in implementing Agile), and Stakeholders (individuals or groups who have a vested interest in the outcome of the project).

It's vital that everyone understands and plays their role correctly as the success of Agile project management largely depends on the effective collaboration of these roles.

## Agile Project Management Process for Organizational Agility

![Agile project management workflow featuring roles, meetings, sprints, and product increments.](/images/blog/Agile-PM-Process-Illustration.jpg)

### The Agile Project Management Process

The Agile project management process is an [iterative and incremental approach](/blog/5-levels-iterative-planning-infographic) to planning and guiding project processes. This dynamic process involves a series of activities that occur at regular intervals with continuous evaluation and adjustments.

### Example--Scrum Project Management Workflow
The [Scrum framework](/blog/overview-of-the-scrum-framework), for example, has an Agile process commences with the Product Owner outlining the [Product Backlog](/blog/5-factors-to-help-prioritize-your-scrum-product-backlog) - a comprehensive list of product requirements ranked based on their business value or priority. This forms the basis of the work to be undertaken by the Agile team.

Following this, the team moves into a [Sprint Planning meeting](/blog/sprint-planning-cheat-sheet) where they determine the work that can be accomplished in the next sprint, which typically ranges from one to four weeks. The selected tasks form the Sprint Backlog.

Every day of the sprint, there is a short meeting known as the [Daily Scrum or Standup](/blog/daily-scrums-awesome) where the team reviews progress and discusses any potential blocks to progress. Here's a breakdown of some of the key events with the Scrum framework:

Key Events for Successful Scrum Project Management

- Sprint Planning: The product owner and the team agree on the work to be done during the upcoming sprint; the team [estimates the effort required](/blog/enhance-scrum-team-performance).
- Daily Scrum or Standup: These [brief meetings](/blog/daily-scrum-cheat-sheet) help the team stay on the same page, discussing what was completed the previous day, what will be done today, and any obstacles that need to be addressed.
- Backlog Refinement: It's a continuous process to review, prioritize, and estimate the size of items in the backlog which is why [backlog refinement as a standard Scrum event](/blog/backlog-refinement-first-class-citizen) helps to avoid unwanted surprises.
- Sprint Review: At the end of each sprint, the team [presents the completed work](/blog/sprint-review-is-more-than-a-demo) to the stakeholders, including a demonstration of the new features.
- Sprint Retrospective: Post the sprint review, the team holds a [retrospect meeting](/blog/a-tried-and-true-method-for-retrospectives) to reflect on the sprint and identify areas for improvement.

## 5 Benefits of Agile for Your Business Transformation Strategy

Agile project management comes with numerous benefits, which make it a preferred choice for many organizations. By focusing on collaboration and flexibility, it enables teams to respond quickly to changes and deliver high-value outcomes.

![Agile project management benefits: flexibility, improved quality, faster delivery, and stakeholder engagement.](/images/blog/Project-Management-Benefits-Desktop-e1736440031973.png)

![Agile project management benefits include flexibility, quality, fast delivery, and stakeholder engagement.](/images/blog/Project-Management-Benefits-Mobile-e1736440261485-565x1024.png)

#### Flexibility and Adaptability:
One of the hallmarks of Agile project management is the ability to adapt to changes. Requirements can change during the project lifecycle, and Agile gives you the flexibility to alter the course as needed. This allows the team to modify the project's scope, alter deadlines, and change features based on arising needs or feedback, without causing significant delays or cost overruns.

#### Higher Product Quality:
By breaking down the project into manageable units, the team can [focus on high-quality development](/blog/qualitative-and-quantitative-measures-infographic), testing, and collaboration. Functionalities are developed and tested in shorter cycles, allowing bugs, inconsistencies, or lack of features to be discovered and corrected early on in the process. This iterative process assures the final product meets quality standards, customer expectations, and market needs--bringing significant benefit not just to the customer but also to the business.

#### Faster Delivery:
Agile's time-boxed, iterative approach allows for more frequent releases and [faster product delivery](/blog/the-need-for-speed-fast-product-delivery-business-agility-2). Each Sprint results in a [potentially shippable product increment (PSPI)](/blog/potentially-shippable-product-increment-pspi-rosetta-stone-software-development), allowing the business to realize value and collect user feedback early and often. Likewise, by breaking down the project into manageable chunks, Agile allows teams to spot issues early and rectify them quickly. This boosts productivity and reduces wasted effort.

#### Stakeholder Engagement:
Agile stresses active user involvement and [collaboration between all stakeholders](/blog/stakeholder-engagement-strategies-agile) with multiple opportunities for stakeholder and team engagement before, during, and after each Sprint. This ongoing interaction ensures that the team understands the stakeholder's viewpoint, reduces the risk of non-compliance with requirements, and improves customer satisfaction. This leads to a team which is highly motivated and better equipped for success.

#### Continuous Improvement:
The Agile process [promotes introspective analysis](/blog/mastering-continuous-improvement-in-agile), including regular check-ins, reviews, and retrospectives. [This feedback loop](/blog/product-quality-with-agile-feedback-loops) allows the team to continually identify areas of weakness, develop action plans to improve, and evolve their tools, skills, processes, and work product.

In essence, Agile project management fosters a [culture of high performance and innovation](/blog/craft-a-culture-of-continuous-improvement), empowering teams to deliver maximum value to their customers. This flexible and customer-centric approach bodes well not only with a rapidly changing market but also with [team motivation and morale](insights/blog/enhance-psychological-safety).

The Real Problem

Your estimates are fiction. Everyone knows it, but nobody says it. Leadership sets dates based on wishful thinking. Teams pad their estimates because they know scope will creep. And then everyone acts surprised when deadlines slip—again.

The result: broken trust, constant firefighting, and leadership that can't make commitments to customers, investors, or the board. Your organization's credibility is eroding one missed deadline at a time.

Signs You Have This Problem

Major releases routinely slip by weeks or months—and no one is surprised anymore

Scope creep is the norm—"just one more thing" adds up to missed deadlines

Estimates are based on gut feel, not data—and they're consistently wrong

Teams pad estimates because they know the original scope won't hold

Leadership has stopped trusting engineering timelines

Every deadline requires heroics—late nights, weekends, and burnout

How We Fix It

Predictability isn't magic—it's math. We help you understand your actual capacity, manage scope realistically, and build forecasting systems based on real data instead of wishful thinking.

01

Establish Real Baselines

We measure how work actually flows through your system. Not how fast you wish you were—how fast you actually are. You can't predict what you don't measure.

02

Fix the Scope Problem

Scope creep kills deadlines. We implement rigorous scope management—not by saying "no" to everything, but by making tradeoffs visible and intentional.

03

Build Forecasting Capability

We implement probabilistic forecasting based on historical data. Know not just when you might deliver, but the confidence level of that date. Give stakeholders ranges they can trust.

What You'll Get

Accurate forecasts. Know when you'll deliver—with 85%+ accuracy. Make commitments you can keep.

Restored trust. When you hit your dates consistently, leadership starts believing you again.

Controlled scope. Scope still changes—but now it's intentional, visible, and properly negotiated.

Sustainable pace. Stop relying on heroics. Consistent delivery without burnout.
